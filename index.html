The Problem

Financial literacy isn't taught — and the consequences are real

Most schools spend years teaching algebra, literature, and history — but nothing about how a payslip works, what a credit score is, or how to avoid falling into debt. The result is that financial confidence and wealth tend to pass through families rather than being equally accessible to everyone.

📉
Debt cycles
High-interest debt traps millions of people who were never taught how compound interest works against them — or how to avoid it in the first place.
🏦
Missed opportunities
Billions in unclaimed benefits, employer pension matches, and ISA allowances go untouched every year — simply because people don't know they exist.
🌍
Inequality of access
Financial knowledge is currently distributed unevenly — passed down through families and social networks. That advantage should be available to everyone, not just some.
🎓
Taught too late
Where financial education exists in schools, it often arrives after the habits are already formed — and rarely covers the things that actually matter in adult life.

Rohan Jain
Creator of FinLit Pro · Sophomore student, Singapore

Hi, I'm Rohan — a sophomore student based in Singapore with a genuine passion for finance and economics. I created FinLit Pro to tackle one of the most overlooked problems in education today: the near-total absence of financial literacy in schools.

The consequences of this gap are serious. Poor financial decision-making is a documented contributor to cycles of debt, poverty, and economic inequality worldwide — yet most people reach adulthood never having been taught how a payslip works, what a credit score is, or how compound interest quietly shapes their future.

FinLit Pro is my attempt to change that. It is completely free, and always will be. The content is designed to be substantive — not a surface-level glossary, but real explanations of how money actually works, tailored to where you are in life. If this helps even one person understand their money a little better, it was worth building.
